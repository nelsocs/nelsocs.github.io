🔥 After Sous Vide

  • Cool in an ice bath for 5 min (prevents internal overcooking).
  • Pat completely dry with paper towels (moisture prevents a good sear).
  • Rest in the fridge for 15 min on a wire rack.
  • Sear on a ripping-hot cast iron skillet for 30-45 sec per side.
  • Serve immediately — no resting required!

Bath temperature = your target doneness. In sous vide, you set the bath to the exact final temperature you want. The meat will equilibrate to this temperature and cannot exceed it. This eliminates carryover cooking and overcooking.

Why do higher temperatures show the same minimum time? Because minimum cook time is bound by physics: the speed of heat diffusion through meat (thermal diffusivity). Above 140°F, pasteurization occurs in a few minutes, but heat still requires the same amount of time to travel to the core. Only thickness and starting state limit the minimum heating time.

Can I speed it up? Yes, by setting a higher bath (e.g. 160°F) and removing the steak when a probe thermometer detects the center has reached 135°F. However, this is no longer hands-off and you risk overcooking the outer layers of the steak. The Baldwin method ensures a perfect edge-to-edge finish.

About This Sous Vide Steak Calculator

This calculator removes the guesswork from cooking steak sous vide by providing precise times based on the thickness, starting state (fresh vs. frozen), steak cut, and your safety preferences. Rather than generic guidelines, it models heat transfer based on the thermal dynamics of meat, combined with pasteurization hold times.

Calculations are derived primarily from Dr. Douglas Baldwin's Practical Guide to Sous Vide Cooking, the gold standard reference in food science. Pasteurization hold times above 140°F (60°C) are computed using Baldwin's z-value logarithmic extrapolation (z=7°C for Listeria in beef).
